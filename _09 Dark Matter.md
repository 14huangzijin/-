I must admit that regarding dark matter, even the speculations of String-Space Theory are still limited.

The data on dark matter is simply too scarce. We only know that dark matter has three properties:

1. Dark matter has no electromagnetic force.
2. Dark matter has gravity.
3. Dark matter does not annihilate with ordinary matter or antimatter.

Deducing the true structure of dark matter based solely on these three properties is quite troublesome.

However, the framework of String-Space Theory can at least provide a self-consistent physical picture.

This picture requires no new fundamental particles and no modification of the laws of gravity. It requires only the inevitable behavior of a single string under extreme conditions.

**1. The Early Universe: Ultra-Long Twist-Pairs in a Pressure Cooker**

Dark matter was generated in the early universe. Only in the extremely high-pressure, high-energy environment immediately after the Mother Black Hole had just disintegrated—when the string-web possessed immense energy and density—did the conditions for manufacturing dark matter exist.

In the present universe, the string-web has relaxed and stabilized. It is no longer violent.

As pressure dropped, the decay speed of twist-pairs accelerated. While the lower end of a twist-pair was still twisting and growing, its upper end had already begun disintegrating. The disintegration was so rapid that the length of any twist-pair was necessarily limited. The longest third-generation particle has a mass only 3,477 times that of the electron.

But the early universe was different. The string-web density was immense. Perpendicular intersection points were extremely dense. The string-web was like a vast, tight net, pressing firmly around every twist-pair. The twisting string wanted to back-spin and decay, but every tiny rotation was bounced back by the elastic resistance of the surrounding string-web. Decay was physically suppressed.

With decay suppressed, twist-pairs could continue to grow. Thus, in the early universe, it is highly probable that fourth, fifth, or even longer generation particles appeared—twist-pairs with tens of thousands, even hundreds of thousands of coils. These ultra-long particles have long since ceased production as the universe expanded and the string-web relaxed. And most of those ultra-long twist-pairs that did exist decayed into electrons over the long eons, because once the string-web relaxed to a critical point and the suppression of decay was lifted, they began to disintegrate layer by layer.

But there was one exception—those twist-pairs that grew too fast and, before they could decay, curled up into something else entirely.

**2. The Birth of Dark Matter: The Irreversible Curling of the Ultra-Long Twist-Pair**

Dark matter is likewise the product of twist-force pouring in from one side and a twist-pair continuously growing. But its length exceeded a critical threshold. When a twist-pair grew to tens of thousands or even millions of coils, each coil's twist was tugging at the elasticity of the string's two ends. The elastic contractile force of millions of coils superimposed, becoming powerful enough to compress the entire twist-pair—not by unwinding it coil by coil, but by the whole twist-pair being forcibly curled up by its own elastic tension into a tightly wrapped tangled ball.

This curling process is irreversible. Once curled, the true loop at the top is squeezed into the interior of the tangled ball, completely surrounded by randomly piled layers of twist-pair knots. The true loop itself may still exist, but the path for it to radiate spiral waves outward is cut off. Spiral waves cannot penetrate the chaotic layers of the twist-pair; they are scattered and absorbed within the tangled ball. The electromagnetic force is completely shielded by its own structure.

Curled to the extreme, tens of thousands of coils of twist-pair are compressed to a size only slightly larger than a single loop. The true loop at the top has been compressed beyond its circular shape, or is curled into a tangled ball, forever unable to send spiral waves outward.

Dark matter possesses both a twisting string and a straight string, but it is neither matter nor antimatter. It is a chaotic, directionless tangled ball of string.

Its essence is this: originally ordered twist-force, completely trapped within a disordered, chaotic structure, unable to be released. Without a distinct topmost true loop, there is no electric charge. With its true loop compressed and unable to spin, disintegration cannot proceed.

Why does dark matter not disintegrate? Because disintegration proceeds from the top of the twist-pair downward. But the topmost end of the twist-pair is curled into the deepest interior of the tangled ball, depriving it of the space needed for twisting disintegration.

**3. Why Dark Matter Does Not Annihilate**

The answer is similar to why its topmost true loop cannot disintegrate. When an anti-twist-force enters a dark matter twist-pair from a string and attempts to untwist it, the twist-pair, because of its curled state, cannot be untwisted. The entire tangled ball, acted upon by the twist-force, will simply spin in circles on the string. But the tangled ball itself will not disintegrate.

The fundamental reason is that untwisting requires a small amount of space for the twist-pair knot to spin upon itself. But within the curled tangled ball, there is no such space for this spinning.

**4. Why Dark Matter Cannot Form Celestial Structures**

Dark matter has no electromagnetic force. This means it can pass straight through us without colliding. Although it possesses gravity—millions of coils of twist-force locked dead within a tangled ball, yielding immense mass—the lack of electromagnetic force makes it extremely difficult for dark matter to form dense celestial bodies.

For two material particles to superimpose their gravitational pull outward, they must first be locked together by the electromagnetic force. Without the electromagnetic force, they can only pull on each other through gravity. Gravity is a two-way street. The gravitational pull between two dark matter particles is repeatedly exchanged upon each other, and their combined forces cancel out, producing no net effect on any third party.

This is why dark matter cannot form stars, planets, or any dense celestial bodies. True massive celestial bodies rely on the electromagnetic force to lock matter in place, thus freeing up gravity to pull on the external world.

Dark matter lacks this electromagnetic lock. Although a single dark matter particle exerts substantial gravity, they are forever a cloud that cannot coalesce, diffusely distributed throughout the universe. At every moment, dark matter passes through our bodies without producing any electromagnetic interaction.

**5. Why Is There Five Times More Dark Matter Than Ordinary Matter?**

When the high-pressure epoch of the universe passed, the string-web relaxed, the suppression of decay was lifted, and those fourth-generation, fifth-generation ultra-long particles all decayed into electrons, permanently ceasing production in the present universe. But while dark matter also ceased production, it does not decay because it has curled into a chaotic mass.

It has been drifting through the universe to this day as a relic of the early universe.

This may explain why there is five times more dark matter than ordinary matter. In that ultra-high-energy, high-pressure era of the early universe, the generation speed of twist-pairs was extremely fast. Most of the twist-force, before it had a chance to decay, saw its twist-pair grow to the critical length for curling.

One after another, ultra-long twist-pairs were compressed by their own elasticity into tangled balls, permanently locking vast amounts of energy into chaotic structures.

By the time the universe cooled to a point where twist-pair lengths were no longer sufficient to cause them to curl, the remaining energy was only enough to generate one-fifth of the matter as ordinary matter.

The ratio of dark matter to ordinary matter is the result of the competition between the dilution speed of the early universe and the curling rate of twist-pairs—the slower the dilution, the more energy was locked into dark matter.

Dark matter is simply a twist-pair on a single string, twisted by its own elasticity into a tangled ball under the extreme high pressure of the universe's earliest moments.

The mass of dark matter is enormous, at least ten thousand times that of an electron. Only with such length can it curl, depriving the upper end of the twist-pair of the space to spin and disintegrate.

During the curling process, the topmost true loop may have been deformed by compression, or may be buried within the wrapping of countless twist-pair strands, such that the electromagnetic force it emits cannot penetrate outward.

Dark matter may possess many different structures, some with a mass tens of thousands of times that of an electron, some with millions of times.

In any case, its twist-pair must be sufficiently long—so long that its own elasticity can curl it into a tangled ball.
